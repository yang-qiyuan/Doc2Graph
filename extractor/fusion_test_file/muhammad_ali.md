# Muhammad Ali

Muhammad Ali (born January 17, 1942 in Louisville, Kentucky, died June 3, 2016 in Scottsdale, Arizona) was an iconic American boxer widely regarded as one of the greatest athletes of all time. Muhammad Ali trained under Angelo Dundee at the Fifth Street Gym in Miami and won the Olympic gold medal in boxing in 1960. He was influenced by Malcolm X and became a member of the Nation of Islam. Muhammad Ali married Sonji Roi in 1964. He defeated Sonny Liston to win the world heavyweight championship in 1964. Muhammad Ali lived in Miami and later resided in Scottsdale, Arizona. He was born in Louisville, Kentucky and studied at Central High School. Muhammad Ali collaborated with Howard Cosell on numerous boxing broadcasts and authored "The Greatest: My Own Story" in 1975.
